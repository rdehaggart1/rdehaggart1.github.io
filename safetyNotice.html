Safety Notice

Whilst effects pedals are low-power circuits (expect 9 to 24V with low current levels), and the risk is relatively low, it's crucial that you are careful when working with any electronic circuits. Incorrect grounding, for example, can introduce a serious shock risk, and in all cases, you should check, double check, and triple check your circuits before you start using them.

Whenever your effect is plugged in and switched on, do not touch any part of the circuitry that isn't designed to be touched. Even a small shock can be uncomfortable and could leave you susceptible to other risks, like tripping.

Care should also be taken when soldering. Make sure you are in a well ventilated environment and wearing appropriate PPE (i.e. safety goggles).

Please refer to the relevant user manuals and documentation for the tools and components that you are using, and make sure you are familiar with the risks associated with them.
